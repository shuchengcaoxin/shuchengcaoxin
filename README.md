### Quantitative Life Sciences PhD @ McGill | Statistical & Causal Genetics | Mendelian Randomization | AI Agents for Drug-Target Discovery

I am a PhD candidate at **McGill University** in [Quantitative Life Sciences](https://www.mcgill.ca/qls/), working at the intersection of **statistical genetics, causal inference, and AI agents** over biomedical data. I use human-genetics evidence to separate true causal drivers from noise — and I build LLM agents that turn many public databases into cited, decision-ready evidence for drug targets.

- 🧬 I work on the **causal genetics of obesity & metabolic disease**
- 🎯 I use **Mendelian randomization & colocalization** to nominate and de-risk **drug targets**
- 🤖 I build **LLM tool-calling agents** over public genetics & safety databases
- 💊 Built my own **drug-target AI agent** end-to-end and mentored a teammate to a working one — **CABS** (Summer 2026)
- 🔬 I bridge **large-scale computational genetics** and **hands-on translational research**
- 📍 Based in Montréal, Canada

---

### 🚀 Featured Projects

🧬 ***[CausalSentinel](https://github.com/ds4cabs/CausalSentinel)*** — an LLM agent that, given a protein + disease, autonomously queries **8 public genetics & safety databases** (UniProt, Open Targets, ChEMBL, ClinVar, gnomAD, GWAS Catalog, PharmGKB, IEU OpenGWAS) and writes a **cited causal-evidence card with a go/no-go verdict**.
